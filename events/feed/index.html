]]> 101: Obstructing The Transition https://cop26coalition.org/events/101-obstructing-the-transition/ Fri, 06 Nov 2020 18:02:50 +0000 https://cop26coalition.org/?post_type=ajde_events&p=2106 Join here: https://zoom.us/j/99099572741

We rapidly have to phase-out fossil fuels but there is a major obstacle for the clean energy transition that hardly anyone knows about: There are more than 2,600 investment treaties and trade agreements that protect foreign investors’ assets, including fossil fuel investments. They enable foreign investors, including shareholders, to sue states over measures that harm their profits, even for climate policies. They can claim vast amounts in compensation.

Several fossil fuel companies have filed or threatened compensation claims against states. For example, the owner of a coal-fired power station in the Netherlands is claiming compensation for the Dutch coal phase-out law. TransCanada demanded $15 billion from the US for halting the Keystone XL oil pipeline.

The list of cases is long and very likely to grow as states are putting their climate commitments into practice. What could this mean for the coal, oil and gas phase-out? What can be done to break the power of fossil fuel firms to obstruct the transition to clean energy?

Covid-19 has brought into consciousness the deep pre-existing health inequalities. It has highlighted how our health is shaped by the wider social, economic and environmental conditions in which we live. At the roots of health injustice is an extractive, economic system that serves to concentrate power in the pursuit of profit through the exploitation of people and the planet. This is the same system that is driving climate breakdown.

This panel discussion seeks to reflect on the connections between health and the climate, the public health impacts of extractive industries and how the Green New Deal can also be a Public Health New Deal.
We will be hearing from voices across the world as to how we build the health movement for transformative climate justice. There will be space for questions and deeper reflection for how we organise from the ground up in the lead up to COP 26. Join us as we explore why health justice means climate justice.

Forest communities worldwide are on the frontlines of protecting and conserving our forests and of struggling against the impacts of monoculture tree plantations, tree burning in power stations for bioenergy, the promotion of ‘nature-based solutions’ by big polluters and other false solutions.

Please join Biofuelwatch and Global Forest Coalition for this session linking struggles and campaigns from groups around the world to protect forests, wildlife, communities and the climate from bioenergy and monoculture tree plantations. The speakers will give information about linking communities who are facing threats to forests from different countries and regions. We will also discuss how community groups in different countries are implementing gender-responsive and rights-based approaches as alternatives to plantations and tree burning and how these approaches can help achieve climate, social and environmental justice.

We’ll have time for discussion and a Q & A with the speakers. Everyone is welcome!
